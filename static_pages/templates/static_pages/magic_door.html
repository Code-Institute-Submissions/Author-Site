For Ava moving to a new town, starting a new school, and learning how to run a hotel already made this summer one of the most exciting she could remember. Little did she know however, that just around the corner, at the bottom of a long, winding and ancient staircase, she would discover a friendship that would cross time itself...

A smart and resourceful 11 year old, Ava had traveled a long way from her native England to the beautiful and picturesque town of Sommières, with her eccentric family and her trusty guinea pig Mr Waffles. Nestled among the striking foothills of the alps and surrounded by glistening vineyards and flowing rivers, was their new home; a rambling old hotel filled with secret corridors and mysterious rooms. It was a dream come true for any budding adventurer.

Yet a chance discovery deep in the hidden, dusty bowels of her new home revealed a mysterious doorway to adventures beyond anything Ava could possibly have imagined.

A glowing portal to times long since passed... Whatever discoveries await beyond, be they magic and wonder or mystery and danger, there would be only one way to find out.

To step through...

“Ava and the Magic Door started off as one of those ideas you have while visiting somewhere really amazing. I knew I wanted to write something, but I wasn't sure how it would all fit together or even what the story would be, I just knew that there would be a story. We were on holiday at the time, staying in this amazing Hotel, the one that Ava lives in, and here i was running around taking as many pictures as I could and scribbling notes all over the place. And then nothing. I let it sit for about a year, like coffee slowly percolating in the back of my brain somewhere. Once day I woke up and BAM there it was, the story.

I sat down at my computer to write, and three months later I had a completed first draft! The more I wrote the more I wanted to write, and the more ideas I had. At a certain point it almost felt like the story was writing itself, and all I could do was hang on and try not to get finger cramp as it used me to escape!”

- Holly Thomas
